Room for Debate

Thursday 10 September · 16:45–18:00 · Two parallel rooms · Two prompts per room · 30 minutes each

These sessions bring together pairs of scholars to deliberate on contested questions in inequality research — exposing the audience to competing perspectives and opening space for the research agendas that emerge when scholars engage directly with one another’s arguments.

Lecture Theatre
Moderated by Selçuk Bedük
Prompt 1
Francisco Ferreira & Franziska Disslbacher
“Intergenerational mobility versus equality of opportunity: Which framework offers a more informative lens for understanding inherited disadvantage?”
Prompt 2
David Brady & Zachary Parolin
“Do Europe and the UK need a new poverty measure? A discussion on whether the at-risk-of-poverty indicator based on 60 percent of median income is still fit for purpose.”
Butler Room
Moderated by Juliana de Castro Galvao
Prompt 3
Salvatore Morelli & Mike Brewer
“Are inequality researchers too concerned with the ultra-rich? A discussion on the economic, social, and political relevance of studying the top of the income and wealth distributions.”
Prompt 4
Arun Advani & Kristoffer Berg
“Wealth taxes: A discussion of the feasibility of the wealth tax in the UK, and how an ideal wealth tax could be designed and administered.”
